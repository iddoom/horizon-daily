## [European Politician Investigating Spyware Industry Hacked with Pegasus](https://techcrunch.com/2026/07/02/politician-who-investigated-spyware-abuses-had-his-phone-hacked-with-pegasus-spyware/) ⭐️ 8.0/10

A European politician serving on an EU committee tasked with investigating the spyware industry was hacked using NSO Group's Pegasus spyware by a government customer. The targeting of a sitting politician actively investigating surveillance abuses represents a direct attack on a democratic oversight process. This incident demonstrates how commercial spyware is being weaponized against the very democratic institutions meant to provide oversight, creating a chilling effect on accountability. It highlights the ongoing failure of existing regulatory frameworks to control the spread and abuse of surveillance technology by government actors. Pegasus is a military-grade spyware capable of zero-click installation, allowing operators to secretly access a device's microphone, camera, messages, and location data without the victim's knowledge. NSO Group claims it only sells to authorized governments for fighting crime and terrorism, but the technology has been repeatedly linked to the surveillance of journalists, activists, and political figures worldwide.

rss · TechCrunch · Jul 3, 05:05

**Background**: NSO Group is an Israeli cyber-intelligence firm that developed Pegasus, a highly sophisticated spyware suite that can be covertly and remotely installed on iOS and Android devices. While the company markets Pegasus as a tool for combating terrorism and crime, investigations by organizations like Citizen Lab and Amnesty International have revealed its widespread use against journalists, lawyers, and political dissidents. In 2021, the Pegasus Project investigation exposed a leaked list of 50,000 phone numbers reportedly selected for targeting by Pegasus customers, drawing global condemnation and lawsuits from companies like Apple and Meta.
