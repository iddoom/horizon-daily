## [European Politician Investigating Spyware Industry Hacked with Pegasus](https://techcrunch.com/2026/07/02/politician-who-investigated-spyware-abuses-had-his-phone-hacked-with-pegasus-spyware/) ⭐️ 8.0/10

A European politician serving on an EU committee tasked with investigating the spyware industry was confirmed to have been hacked using NSO Group's Pegasus spyware by a government customer. The targeting occurred while the politician was actively engaged in oversight work examining abuses within the commercial surveillance sector. This incident represents a direct attack on democratic oversight, demonstrating how commercial spyware can be weaponized to undermine the very institutions meant to regulate surveillance technology. It highlights the severe chilling effect on public accountability when those investigating abuses become targets themselves, raising urgent questions about the unchecked power of the spyware industry. Pegasus is capable of zero-click attacks that require no interaction from the phone's owner, making it extremely difficult to detect or defend against. NSO Group has consistently claimed it only sells its technology to government clients for fighting crime and terrorism, but numerous investigations have documented its use against journalists, activists, and political figures worldwide.

rss · TechCrunch · Jul 3, 05:05

**Background**: Pegasus is a proprietary spyware developed by NSO Group, an Israeli cyber-intelligence firm founded by Niv, Shalev, and Omri. The spyware can remotely infect both iOS and Android devices through zero-click exploits or by exploiting zero-day vulnerabilities in operating systems, granting attackers full access to messages, calls, camera, microphone, and stored data. The EU has been conducting investigations into the spyware industry following revelations that multiple European governments used Pegasus to surveil political opponents, journalists, and other civilians, prompting concerns about democratic backsliding and human rights violations.
